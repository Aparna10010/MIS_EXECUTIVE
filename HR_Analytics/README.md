# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard | Employee Attrition Analysis (Excel)

## 🔍 Project Overview

This project presents an **interactive HR Analytics dashboard** built in Microsoft Excel to analyze **employee attrition patterns**. It uncovers key factors driving employee exits and provides **data-driven recommendations** to improve retention.

The analysis focuses on critical dimensions such as **job role, overtime, salary, promotions, and work-life balance**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze employee attrition trends and patterns
* Identify high-risk departments and employee segments
* Evaluate impact of overtime, salary, and promotions
* Provide actionable recommendations to improve retention

---

## 📊 Dataset

* **Source:** IBM HR Analytics Dataset
* **Total Records:** 1,400+ employees
* **Key Features:**

  * Job Role
  * Salary (Income Group)
  * Overtime
  * Work-Life Balance
  * Promotion History
  * Attrition Status

---

## 📌 Key Insights

* 📈 **~40% attrition in Sales roles**, indicating high pressure and low retention
* 🔥 Employees working overtime show **3x higher attrition (30.5% vs 10.4%)**
* 💸 **Low-income group has the highest attrition**, highlighting financial stress
* 📉 **~17% attrition among recently promoted employees**, suggesting lack of support
* ⚖️ Poor work-life balance strongly correlates with higher attrition

---

## 🛠️ Recommendations

* 🎯 Improve onboarding and mentorship for Sales roles
* ⏱️ Reduce overtime through workload optimization or hiring
* 💰 Provide benefits (WFH, allowances, skill development) for low-income employees
* 📈 Introduce post-promotion training and regular check-ins
* 🧠 Enhance work-life balance with flexible policies and structured work hours

---

## 🚀 Conclusion

The analysis reveals that **employee attrition is driven more by burnout, lack of support, and limited growth opportunities than salary alone**. Implementing targeted HR strategies can significantly improve employee retention and organizational performance.
